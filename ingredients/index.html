Ingredient Guide

Korean skincare ingredients, explained simply.

You do not need to memorize every ingredient to build a good routine. Start with a few proven basics, match them to your skin type, and introduce new products slowly.

Niacinamide

Helps support a more even-looking tone, balances oil, and strengthens the skin barrier. It is one of the easiest beginner ingredients to use.

Best for: oily, combination, uneven-looking skin

Hyaluronic Acid

A hydration ingredient that helps skin hold water. It works best when followed with moisturizer to seal hydration in.

Best for: dry, dehydrated, tight-feeling skin

Centella Asiatica

A calming ingredient often used in Korean skincare for redness-prone or sensitive skin. It pairs well with gentle barrier-focused routines.

Best for: sensitive, irritated, barrier-damaged skin

Snail Mucin

A lightweight hydrating ingredient that can make skin feel smoother and more comfortable. Patch test first if you are allergy-prone.

Best for: dry texture, dullness, post-breakout care

Ceramides

Barrier-supporting lipids that help reduce dryness and strengthen the skin's protective layer. They are especially useful when skin feels over-cleansed.

Best for: dry, sensitive, damaged skin barriers

Retinol

A stronger active ingredient that supports smoother texture over time. Beginners should use it at night, start slowly, and avoid layering it with too many actives.

Best for: texture, early signs of aging, uneven tone

What beginners should avoid mixing

Most irritation happens when too many strong products are introduced at once. Keep your routine boring at first.

  • Do not start retinol, exfoliating acids, and vitamin C in the same week.
  • Do not exfoliate every day.
  • Do not use a strong active when your skin barrier feels tight, burning, or flaky.
  • Always use sunscreen in the morning when using exfoliants or retinol.
