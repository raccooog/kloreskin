Double Cleansing: Do You Actually Need It?

It's the most-debated step in Korean skincare — some people swear by it, others think it's overkill. Here's what it's actually for, so you can decide for your own skin.

K Kloré Skin • Updated July 20, 2026 • 5 min read

Quick answer: Double cleansing means using an oil-based cleanser first to dissolve sunscreen, makeup, and sebum, followed by a water-based cleanser to clean the skin itself. You need it on days you wore sunscreen or makeup. On bare-skin days, a single gentle cleanser is enough — double cleansing every single day can strip and irritate sensitive skin.

What Double Cleansing Is

Double cleansing is a two-step cleansing method, not a single product. Step one is an oil-based cleanser (oil, balm, or micellar water) that breaks down sunscreen, SPF, and makeup. Step two is a gentle, water-based cleanser that removes sweat, sebum, and whatever the first step left behind.

The idea comes from a simple principle: oil dissolves oil. A water-based cleanser alone often can't fully break down sunscreen or long-wear makeup, which is why residue can linger even after you've "washed your face."

Why It Works

It Actually Removes Sunscreen

Modern sunscreens, especially water-resistant ones, are built to cling to skin. An oil cleanser breaks that bond far more effectively than a foaming cleanser used on its own.

It Prevents Clogged Pores

Leftover sunscreen and sebum mixed together is a common, overlooked cause of clogged pores and dullness — not just skipping cleansing altogether.

It Preps Skin for the Rest of Your Routine

A properly cleansed face lets toners, serums, and moisturizers absorb the way they're meant to, instead of sitting on top of leftover residue.

Do You Actually Need It?

Your Day Double Cleanse? Why
Wore sunscreen or makeup Yes A single cleanser usually can't fully remove it
Sweated heavily / worked out Yes Clears mixed sebum and residue before it settles in pores
Bare skin, no products all day Not necessary One gentle cleanser is enough — no buildup to remove
Very dry or reactive skin Only when needed Daily double cleansing can be too stripping; scale back if skin feels tight

If your skin already feels tight, red, or reactive after cleansing, it may be a barrier issue rather than a cleansing issue — our centella asiatica (cica) guide covers how to calm that down.

How to Double Cleanse the Right Way

Two Steps

  • ✔ Step 1: Oil or balm cleanser — massage over dry skin for 60 seconds, then rinse or wipe off
  • ✔ Step 2: Gentle, low-pH water-based cleanser — cleanse as normal

Follow with your usual routine: toner, treatment, moisturizer, and sunscreen in the morning.

The oil step should feel like a massage, not a scrub — let the oil do the work instead of rubbing hard. Rinse with lukewarm water; hot water strips more than it cleans.

Common Mistakes

  • Double cleansing every day regardless of whether you wore sunscreen or makeup.
  • Using two harsh, foaming cleansers back to back instead of oil + gentle cleanser.
  • Scrubbing hard during the oil step instead of massaging gently.
  • Skipping it entirely on sunscreen days and wondering why breakouts persist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to double cleanse every day?

Only on days you wore sunscreen, SPF makeup, or sweated heavily. On bare-skin days, one gentle cleanser is enough.

Will double cleansing dry out my skin?

Not if you use a proper oil-based first cleanser and a gentle, low-pH second cleanser. Stripping usually comes from using two harsh cleansers, not the method itself.

Can I double cleanse in the morning too?

Usually not necessary — a single gentle cleanser in the morning is enough, since you haven't worn sunscreen or makeup overnight.

What if I have acne-prone skin?

Double cleansing on sunscreen or makeup days can actually help by fully clearing residue that would otherwise clog pores — just avoid over-cleansing on bare-skin days.

Final Thoughts

Double cleansing isn't a rule you follow every day no matter what — it's a tool for the days your skin actually needs it. Match it to what you wore, not to a fixed routine, and it stops being a debate.
